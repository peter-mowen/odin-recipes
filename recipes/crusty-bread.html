Crusty Bread

Description

Pairs well with preserves and butter, dipping in olive oil, serving with charcuterie, or using as a base for french toast.

This recipe is adapted from the French Bread recipe in Better Homes and Gardens New Cook Book (1981) and the cooking techniques outlined in Flour, Water, Salt, Yeast.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 package active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup warm water (115°F to 120°F)

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of the flour, the yeast, and salt.
  2. Add warm water.
  3. Beat at low speed of electric mixer for ½ minute, scraping sides of bowl constantly.
  4. Beat 3 minutes at high speed.
  5. Stir in as much of the remaining flour as you can mix with a spoon.
  6. Turn out onto a lightly floured surface.
  7. Knead enough remaining flour to make a stiff dough that is smooth and elastic (8 to 10 minutes).
  8. Stretch dough and fold it in half, turn 90°, and repeat stretch, fold, rotate 3 more times for a total of 4 folds.
  9. Shape dough into a ball.
  10. Place in a lighly greased bowl; turn once to grease surface
  11. Cover; let rise in a warm place till double (1 to 1¼ hours).
  12. Punch down; turn out onto a lightly floured surface.
  13. Give 3-4 more folds.
  14. Cover; let rise till nearly double (about 45 minutes).
  15. While dough is rising, put dutch oven with its lid into oven and preheat to 450°F. Leave dutch oven in preheated oven until bread is done rising.
  16. Transfer dough to dutch oven. Put lid on dutch oven. Return to oven and bake for 30 minutes.
  17. Remove dutch oven lid and bake 10 minutes more.
  18. Remove bread from dutch oven and put on wire rack to cool.
