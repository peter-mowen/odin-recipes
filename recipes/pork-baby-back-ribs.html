Pork Baby Back Ribs

Description

Generic recipe for fall-off-the-bone pork baby back ribs done on the grill.

This recipe assumes the use of a charcoal grill (like a Weber kettle grill) but can be adapted to work on a gas grill just as well.

Ingredients

  • 2 racks of pork baby back ribs
  • Yellow Mustard (used as binder)
  • Your favorite rub
  • Melted Butter
  • Brown Sugar
  • Apple Juice
  • Your favorite sauce or a sauce to pair with the rub

Steps

  1. Pat ribs dry.
  2. Remove silver skin from back of ribs.
  3. Rub ribs with Yellow Mustard on top and bottom.
  4. Generously sprinkle both sides of the ribs with your favorite rub. DO NOT RUB IT IN. It will adhere to the yellow mustard and stick to the ribs.
  5. Let ribs rest on a baking sheet in the fridge for at least 4 hours,preferably overnight. If you are short on time, you can let the ribs rest on the counter at room temperature for an hour.
  6. Prepare grill.
    1. Put charcoal in charcoal chimney and light charcoal.
    2. Once charcoal is lit, pour it out of the chimney and push one half to one side and the other half to the other side, leaving a hole in the middle.
    3. Put a foil drip pan in the space between the coals.
    4. Fill drip pan with water.
    5. Put lid on grill and adjust vents so the dome temperature is between 250°F and 300°F.
    6. Optionally, add wood chunks to charcoal for smoky flavor.
  7. Once grill is up to temp, put ribs meat side up in the center of the grill over the drip pan.
  8. Close lid and grill until meat pulls back from ends of ribs, about 2 hours, adding charcoal as needed.
  9. Put each rack of ribs on a piece of heavy duty aluminum foil.
  10. Brush Melted Butter onto meat side of ribs.
  11. Sprinkle meat side of ribs with brown sugar.
  12. Wrap ribs in aluminum foil, leaving one end open.
  13. Pour 4-8 oz of Apple Juice in each foil packet.
  14. Seal foil packets.
  15. Return foil packets to grill, meat side down.
  16. Grill until ribs reach 200°F-210°F.
  17. If adding sauce, unwrap ribs meat side up, apply sauce, and grill for another 10-15 minutes.
  18. Remove ribs from grill and let rest for 10 minutes.
  19. Cut ribs into 1-3 rib sections. Plate and serve.
